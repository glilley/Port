VISION

It all starts with an idea, and in this case, an idea sparked out of necessity and recognition of a need. Airbnb hosts were posting ads on TaskRabbit to fulfill check-in and cleaning services, but results were subpar and not tailored to their needs.

objective

What’s the purpose of the idea? Broadly stated but focused enough to strive for.

Provide services lacking in New York’s short-term rental property market.

goal

What does success look like?

Build a platform that allows busy airbnb users to schedule concierge services for guest bookings in New York City.

RESEARCH

interview

Live conversations trumped all. Even phone meetings were more informative than email because the best nuggets of new information were extracted from tone, expressions, and untailored responses which hinted at new pain points. Comfort and openness were key.

ethnographic research

As a customer-facing service, we needed to see exactly how the hosts were interacting with their guests. We shadowed hosts through entire reservation cycles from booking responses to physical check-in and check-out processes.

finding

Roughly 80% of the unknowns at check-in could be solved with a single well-written and informative email at booking. Also, fewer emails exchanged resulted in better reviews on average.

personas

Referencing imaginary customers is surprisingly useful, likely because they aren’t so imaginary if done right. Developed largely from assumptions but supported by data from ArcGIS, interviews, and airbnb stats, meet Mike and Robin.

market analysis

With few relevant competitors we also looked to traidtional property management companies. We determined a set of critical success factors based on what key pain points were being overlooked as identified from interviews and referenced from our personas.

Having a majority of managed properties as opposed to one-off services reduces communications errors, provides more value for guests, improves workforce quality, and increases revenue.

CONCEPT

solutions

A critical success factor is nice to know but how do you achieve it? Ideate.


More managed properties was a critical success factor so we structured our pricing tiers towards management. Late guests strained resources so we implemented rules and a lockbox pickup.

strategy

Structure ideas into task flows to assess operation cycles. This forces clarity and consideration for each step in the cycle.

identity

Utilize personas and service offerings to create a voice, name, and face. Content should be established before moving on to the purely visual elements or even the wireframes because how you want to sound should ultimately determine how you present the idea.

Personalized, convenient, fresh, trustworthy, and all-inclusive were key words derived from our research that we wanted to portray. To avoid our already bad luck with taken domains we got a little clever and referenced the French word for eight: "huit" — we were four people, so eight arms worth of help is what you get.

CREATE

wireframe

Organization and user flows are loosely sketched for a general idea. Then, a combination of Balsamiq, Sketch, and Illustrator are used to clean up and refine ideas.

A mobile first web app aligned with our technical skills and desire to be cross platform, which current HTML5 wrappers provide.

prototype

Interactions take center stage as the wireframes are organized and linked together for user testing with Balsamiq. Focus is initially on the meat and potatoes — 20% of functions that satisfy 80% of needs. Our key tasks were updating availability, The prototype is void of visual design to focus on the content placement and interactions.

mockup

High fidelity mockups complete with visual design solidify the identity and provide an aesthetic to strive for during the actual build process.


Like with most of the previous steps, visual design is not a specific step down the road. It was thought about throughout the research and concept phases and adjusted as findings and goals changed. With a solid foundation, however, it's the visual design that will tie the pieces together and get everyone excited.

build

Huitly’s web app is still under construction, but a live site is available at www.Huit.ly

iterate

It's likely that some things don’t work as expected (blame the user! Kidding.). Your copy isn’t as clever as you thought because everyone keeps asking what exactly you do or how something works. It's time to adjust.

We’ve added services to our management model that address needs we weren't focusing on the first time around because we were MVP-ing it.
