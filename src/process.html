design process

about

Everyone can solve problems, but designers strategically frame problems to replicate finding solutions for any problem. It’s not always visual, though who doesn’t admire the great work on dribbble, and it’s not always the most creative solution — yes, parallax effects can be cool. The best solutions are those that meet strategic goals and provide a holistically well-thought experience. From creative microcopy that provides a human touch at the end of a digital screen to the subtle feedback a user gets from a slight change in color when an input occurs. These are the details I care about. These are the things I appreciate when done well.

My experience is diverse. From political science degree, to financial auditor, and law student, each stop along the way has had one thing in common: I was communicating ideas, a process, a solution. This is what I do now, but in a new medium.

VISION

It all starts with an idea, and in this case, an idea sparked out of necessity and recognition of a need. Airbnb hosts were posting ads on TaskRabbit to fulfill services, but were finding the results were subpar and not tailored to their needs.

objective

What’s the purpose of the idea? Broadly stated but focused enough to strive for.
Ex: Provide services lacking in New York’s short-term rental property market.

goal

What does success look like?
Ex: Build a platform that allows busy airbnb users to schedule concierge services for guest bookings in New York City.

RESEARCH

interview

Live conversations trumped all. Even phone meetings were more informative than email because the best nuggets of new information were extracted from tone, expressions, and untailored responses which hinted at new pain points. Comfort and openness were key.

ethnographic research (interviews)

As a customer-facing service, we needed to see exactly how the hosts were interacting with their guests. We shadowed hosts through entire reservation cycles from booking responses to physical check-in and check-out processes.

findings

Roughly 80% of the unknowns at check-in could be solved with a single well-written and informative email at booking. Also, fewer emails exchanged resulted in better reviews.

personas

Referencing imaginary customers is surprisingly useful, likely because they aren’t so imaginary if done right. Developed largely from assumptions but backed up with all the data we could conjure. Meet Mike and Robin.

competitive analysis

Examine closest product’s marketing, management, pricing, strengths and weaknesses. We used a pseudo-analysis to determine critical success factors and to define what pain points from our personas were being overlooked.

Ex: Acquiring a majority of managed-property clients as opposed to one-off services reduces communications errors, provides more value for guests, and improves workforce quality.

CONCEPT

solutions

A critical success factor is nice to know but how do you achieve it? Ideate.

strategy

Structure ideas into task flows to assess operation cycles. This forces clarity and consideration for each step in the cycle.

identity

Utilize personas and service offerings to create a voice, name, and face. Content should be established before moving on to the purely visual elements or even the wireframes because how you want to sound should ultimately determine how you present the idea.

We had some placeholder names and even settled on a couple that didn’t have available domain names. It may seem premature to go to these lengths, but as a start-up it provides early legitimacy when pitching to at least have a domain with a company email and logo.

CREATE

wireframe

Architecture and user flows are loosely sketched for a general idea. Then, Balsamiq, Omnigraffle, or Illustrator are used to clean up and refine the sketches. Based on our personas, a mobile first approach was necessary.

prototype

Interactions take center stage as the wireframes are organized and linked together for user testing. Focus is initially on the meat and potatoes - 20% of functions that satisfy 80% of needs. Also, the prototype is void of visual design to focus on the content and interactions.

mockup

High fidelity mockups allow for fine tuning the identity and provide an aesthetic to strive for during the actual build process.

build

Huitly’s web app is still under construction, but a live site is available at www.Huit.ly

iterate

Some things don’t work as expected. Your copy isn’t as clever as you thought because everyone keeps asking what exactly you do or how something works.
Well, it’s time to update or improve. We’ve added services to our management model as we’ve recognized the need and to help us achieve our critical success factors.
